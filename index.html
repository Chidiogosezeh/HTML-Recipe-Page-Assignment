Nigerian Meatpie

Nigerian Meatpie is a savory flaky pastry snack filled with minced meat (commonly beef), and vegetables like potatoes, carrots, and onions. It is a street-food favorite often served at parties, in schools, and as a light meal across Nigeria.

Recipe Details

  • Prep time: 30 minutes
  • Cook time: 30 minutes
  • Servings: 8 meatpies
  • Difficulty: Intermediate

Picture of Nigerian Meatpie

Ingredients

For the Dough:

  • 500g Plain wheat flour
  • 250g Cold margarine
  • 1/4 cup Cold water
  • 1 Egg for egg wash

For the Filling:

  • 3 tablespoons of Vegetable oil
  • 300g minced Meat
  • 3 medium-sized Irish potatoes (diced)
  • 3 medium-sized Carrots (diced)
  • 1 small Onion (chopped)
  • 1 Seasoning cube
  • 1 teaspoon Curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 tablespoon Plain wheat flour for thickening
  • 1/4 cup Water
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Make the filling: In a pan, heat 1 tablespoon vegetable oil over medium heat until shimmering. Add ground beef, season lightly with salt, and cook, stirring and breaking up chunks of meat, until beef is no longer pink, about 5 minutes. Transfer to medium heatproof bowl and set aside.
  2. Add remaining 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil to now-empty pan and heat on medium heat. Add onions, potatoes, and carrot, season with salt, and cook, stirring occasionally, until just beginning to soften, for 5 minutes. Add curry powder and thyme and cook, stirring frequently, until fragrant, for 2 minutes.
  3. Add cooked beef along with the 1/4 cup water, cover, and bring to a boil. Reduce heat to maintain a simmer and cook, stirring once or twice, until vegetables are softened, for 10 minutes.
  4. Sprinkle 1 tablespoon plain wheat flour into the beef mixture to thicken it. Bring to a simmer and cook, stirring occasionally, until beef and vegetables are coated in a thickened sauce, for 5 minutes. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Transfer to medium heatproof bowl and set aside to cool.
  5. Make the dough: In a bowl, add flour and rub in cold margarine until crumbly.
  6. Add cold water gradually and knead until a smooth, non-sticky dough forms. Wrap and rest for 15 minutes, preferably, in a refrigerator.
  7. Roll out dough, cut into circles, brush the edges of the circles with beaten egg, add 2 tablespoons filling to each, fold, and seal edges with a fork.
  8. Preheat oven to 400°F (205°C). When ready to bake, brush surface of pies with beaten egg. Poke pies with a fork, 3 times each. Bake meatpies, rotating tray halfway through, until golden brown on all sides, for 30 minutes. Let cool for 10 minutes.
  9. Transfer meat pies to a serving platter and serve warm or at room temperature.

Tips

Important: Keep margarine and water very cold for a flaky crust. Do not overwork the dough or it becomes tough. Ensure filling is completely cool before adding to dough to prevent sogginess. Filling can be refrigerated in an airtight container for up to 3 days.
